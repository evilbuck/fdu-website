1. **Regional Expertise** - 24 years in Bay Area, deep AHJ relationships, local Concord office

3. **Veteran-Led Reliability** - USMC discipline, family-owned stability, 24/7 availability
4. **Responsive Partnership** - Direct access to leadership, fast permits, seamless inspections

## Customer Personas

### Primary: The General Contractor

**Profile:** Manages commercial construction projects ($500K-$50M+), focused on schedules, budgets, and risk mitigation  
**Age:** 35-55  
**Job Title:** Project Manager, Superintendent, Owner  
**Company Size:** 10-200 employees  
**Decision Criteria:**
- Can they handle Design-Build? (eliminates coordination headaches)
- Will they pass AHJ inspection on first try? (avoids costly delays)
- Are they responsive when problems arise? (keeps project on track)
- Do they understand construction schedules? (integration, not interference)

**Pain Points:**
- Fire alarm delays can halt entire multi-million dollar projects
- Coordinating between separate designer and installer creates liability gaps
- Failed inspections cost days/weeks and damage reputation
- Unresponsive contractors during critical project phases

**Value Proposition:**
> "FDU eliminates fire alarm headaches with unified Design-Build delivery. One point of accountability. Faster permits. First-time inspections. Your project stays on schedule."

**Messaging Keywords:** Design-Build, Value Engineering, Seamless Permitting, Project Schedule, Single Point of Contact, AHJ Coordination

**Content Needs:**
- Case studies of on-time project completions
- Process explanation (how Design-Build works)
- AHJ relationship testimonials
- Quick quote/estimate tool

**Multi-Persona Landing Page Approach:**
Create persona-specific landing pages that can be tested and optimized independently. Each page targets one primary persona with tailored messaging, imagery, and CTAs while maintaining consistent brand elements.

**Homepage:** General overview with clear persona pathways  
**Service Pages:** Detail what you do

## Lead Generation Strategy

### Primary Conversion Points

1. **Quote Request Form** - Primary goal
   - Detailed form on dedicated page
   - Also available as modal/lightbox from CTAs
   - Fields: See above

2. **Phone Call** - Secondary but important
   - Click-to-call throughout site
   - Phone number in header (sticky)
   - Phone number at end of every page
   - Track which pages drive calls

3. **Email/Contact Form** - Tertiary
   - Simple contact form for general inquiries
   - Lower commitment than quote request

### Lead Qualification

**Ideal Lead Criteria:**
- Commercial project (not residential)
- Bay Area or Sacramento region
- New construction, major renovation, or system replacement
- Decision maker or influencer (GC PM, Property Manager, Engineer, Owner)

**Lead Scoring (Internal):**
- Project size/value
- Timeline (sooner = higher score)
- Persona match
- Engagement level (pages visited, time on site)

### Lead Nurturing

**Immediate Response:**
- Auto-confirmation email: "Thanks for your inquiry, we'll respond within 24 hours"
- Internal notification to Rick/sales team

**Follow-up Sequence:**
- Day 1: Personal response from Rick (phone call + email)
- Day 3: Follow-up if no response
- Day 7: Additional follow-up with case study or resource
- Day 14: Final check-in

**Tracking:**
- CRM setup (HubSpot free or similar)
- Lead source tracking (which page/form)
- Conversion tracking (lead → quote → closed deal)

- **Risk:** Website generates leads but they're not qualified
- **Mitigation:** Form field optimization, clear qualifying questions, persona-specific landing pages, Rick feedback loop
